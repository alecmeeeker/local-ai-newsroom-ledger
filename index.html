The Commit&Ledger

I am building AI tooling for small newsrooms. The goal is to let them turn their editorial experience and local knowledge into reporting that is as fast, accurate, and deep as the work coming out of large national outlets. Bushwick Daily uses parts of it now. The chart below is that codebase, plotted day by day.

What This Is

A toolkit, not a newsroom.

This repo is a set of tools I have been building for local-news work. It includes scrapers for regional news sources, a pgvector store that indexes the corpus, a multi-agent pipeline that triages stories and produces draft copy, an editor review UI for human approval, and a publish stack that can post to WordPress and Instagram.

Bushwick Daily uses parts of this toolkit for specific editorial workflows. The publication is not run by the code. The code is one of the tools the publication uses.

I built it. I used Claude Code as my dev partner along the way.

Why I Am Building It

The mission is to save local journalism by giving small newsrooms the tools they need to leverage their human editorial experience and local knowledge, so local reporting can be fast, accurate, and have the depth of the large nationally funded outlets. ALEC MEEKER, ON THE PROJECT

Small newsrooms have the editorial experience and the local knowledge. They do not always have the engineering. I am building these tools to close that gap. Bushwick Daily is the first newsroom using them. As the pieces stabilize I plan to open-source them so other local publishers can adopt the parts that fit their work.
